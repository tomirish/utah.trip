Restaurants
Cedar City, UT · Day 1
  • The Pub Spirits + Craft Kitchen — ★ Day 1 dinner · burgers, craft cocktails, downtown staple · online reservations available
  • Centro Woodfired Pizzeria — Neapolitan-style pizza from a wood-fired brick oven, best restaurant in town
  • Fire + Smoke BBQ — Texas-style brisket, ribs, pulled pork · open Wed–Sat only
  • Hermie's Drive In — Cedar City institution, classic burgers, Navajo tacos, fast and beloved
Bryce Canyon Area · Day 2
  • Stone Hearth Grille — best dinner in the area, locally sourced, reservation recommended · Tropic, ~10 min
  • Ebenezer's Barn & Grill — ★ Day 2 option · western dinner show, live country music, BBQ ribs · tickets required, book ahead
  • Bryce Canyon Pines Restaurant — beloved roadside diner, homestyle food, famous homemade pies
  • Cowboy's Buffet & Steak Room — ★ Day 2 option · on-site at Ruby's, open from 6:30 AM
  • Canyon Diner — ★ Day 2 option · casual counter-service at Ruby's Inn, quick and convenient
Springdale, UT · Days 3–5
  • Porters — ★ Day 3 dinner · top dinner spot in Springdale, canyon wall views
  • Wild Thyme — upscale-ish, seasonal menu, nicest overall spot in town
  • Spotted Dog Café at Flanigan's — ★ Day 4 dinner · locally sourced, great patio · online reservations available
  • Whiptail Grill — ★ Day 5 dinner · Mexican-inspired, great outdoor patio
  • Bit & Spur — Southwestern, strong margaritas, been there since '85
  • Cowboys & Angels — eclectic American, lively bar scene
  • Zion Brew Pub — craft beers, burgers, casual patio vibe
St. George, UT · Days 6–7
  • Wood. Ash. Rye. — best restaurant in St. George, chef-driven seasonal American, Salt Lake Mag Best Restaurant 2024
  • Xetava Gardens Cafe — hidden gem in Kayenta Art Village, gorgeous desert setting · Ivins, ~20 min
  • Riggatti's Wood Fired Pizza — ★ Day 6 dinner (to go) · local institution, artisan pizza, fresh dough, gelato · closed Sundays
  • Positano — upscale Italian, house-made pastas, strong wine list, good for wedding-weekend dinners
Grand Canyon Village, AZ · Days 8–9
  • El Tovar Dining Room — ★ Day 8 dinner · iconic lodge right on the rim, upscale · online reservations available
  • Bright Angel Lodge Restaurant — ★ Day 9 breakfast · casual, steps from the trailhead
  • Maswik Pizza Patio — at your lodge, quick and easy for a low-key night
  • Canyon Village Market & Deli — ★ Day 9 dinner · grab sandwiches and supplies, easy pack-night option
June Tips
  • 🌅 Start every hike by 7 AM — temps hit 90–95°F by midday, most trails have zero shade
  • 💧 2L water per person minimum — bring electrolyte tablets, water alone isn't enough on big days
  • ☀️ SPF 50 sunscreen, reapply every 2 hrs — high elevation + reflective red rock = brutal UV
  • 📱 Download AllTrails offline the night before — cell service in the canyons is essentially zero
  • ⚡ Check NOAA before slot canyon hikes — late June monsoons cause flash floods with no warning. Exit if weather builds
  • 🚌 Zion shuttle is free, runs every 5–10 min — no personal vehicles allowed in Zion Canyon
  • 🧦 Rent wet feet gear for the Narrows — neoprene socks + water shoes + pole · ~$45/person · Zion Outfitter or Zion Guru
