## Hike Details

### 🌊 Kanarra Falls
**Day 2 — Monday June 22** ✅ BOOKED

| | |
|---|---|
| **Distance** | 3.7 miles out-and-back |
| **Difficulty** | Moderate |
| **Time** | 2.5–3 hours |
| **Permit** | Required — $15/person · 150 hikers/day cap |
| **AllTrails** | alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/kanarra-falls |

You hike IN Kanarra Creek through a slot canyon with multiple waterfalls. The canyon narrows to jaw-dropping proportions with water ankle-to-knee deep throughout. At the first major waterfall you climb a 20-foot metal ladder to continue. Scramble over boulders to reach the second waterfall — the turnaround point for day hikers.

**What to bring:** Waterproof shoes or neoprene socks · trekking poles helpful · change of socks  
**⚠️ Book permits immediately at kanarrafalls.com — $15/person — June sells out weeks ahead**

### 🥾 Peekaboo + Navajo Loop + Queen's Garden
**Day 3 — Tuesday June 23 (morning)**

| | |
|---|---|
| **Distance** | 5.5–6 miles |
| **Difficulty** | Strenuous |
| **Time** | 3.5–4.5 hours |
| **Permit** | None required |
| **AllTrails** | alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/navajo-loop-and-queens-garden-trail |

The ultimate Bryce morning. Start at Sunset Point and combine three trails into one loop that goes deeper than most visitors ever venture.

- **Navajo Loop** — descends through Wall Street (Bryce's only slot canyon, walls 20 ft apart with ancient ponderosa pines) · passes Thor's Hammer up close
- **Queen's Garden** — gets you up close to the Queen Victoria hoodoo (genuinely looks like her in profile)
- **Peekaboo Loop** — deeper into the amphitheater · far fewer crowds · massive hoodoo walls overhead · horse traffic from guided rides

**⚠️ Start no later than 7 AM — zero shade, gets hot fast. Bryce sits at 8,000 ft — the altitude will make climbs harder than expected.**

**Day 3 — Tuesday June 23 (en route)**

| | |
|---|---|
| **Distance** | 1.7 miles out-and-back |
| **Difficulty** | Easy |
| **Time** | 20–30 minutes |
| **Cost** | Free |
| **AllTrails** | alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/the-belly-of-the-dragon |

### 🏞️ Canyon Overlook Trail
**Day 3 — Tuesday June 23 (afternoon arrival at Zion)**

| | |
|---|---|
| **Distance** | 1 mile out-and-back |
| **Difficulty** | Easy |
| **Time** | 45 minutes |
| **Permit** | None |
| **AllTrails** | alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/canyon-overlook-trail |

Best easy hike in Zion — highest payoff for least effort. Starts at the east end of the Zion-Mt. Carmel Tunnel. Almost no elevation gain, weaving through potholes and slickrock to a dramatic panoramic view of lower Zion Canyon and the Great Arch. Perfect warm-up for your first afternoon without destroying your legs before the big days ahead.

### 🌊 The Narrows (Bottom-Up)
**Day 4 — Wednesday June 24 (morning)** ✅

| | |
|---|---|
| **Distance** | Up to 9.4 miles out-and-back (go as far as you want) |
| **Difficulty** | Moderate |
| **Time** | 3–5 hours |
| **Permit** | None required for bottom-up day hike |
| **Gear Rental** | Zion Outfitter · ~$45/person · neoprene socks + water shoes + trekking pole |
| **Shuttle Stop** | Temple of Sinawava (last stop) |
| **AllTrails** | alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/zion-narrows-trail-to-imlay-temple-and-big-spring |

One of the most unique hikes in America. The paved Riverside Walk (1 mile flat) leads to where the trail disappears into the river — from here you hike upstream in the Virgin River through a slot canyon with walls up to 1,000 feet tall and just 20–30 feet wide in places. Water is ankle-to-waist deep. Cold but refreshing in June. Go as far as you feel like and turn back.

**⚠️ Flash flood warning: Always check NOAA forecast. Do not enter if storms are forecast anywhere in the watershed — flash floods arrive with no warning.**

**Day 5 — Thursday June 25 (morning) — RECOMMENDED over Angels Landing**

| | |
|---|---|
| **Distance** | 8 miles out-and-back |
| **Difficulty** | Hard |
| **Time** | 4–5 hours |
| **Elevation Gain** | 2,148 ft |
| **Permit** | None required |
| **AllTrails** | alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/observation-point-trail |

You look DOWN on Angels Landing from the summit — many hikers say this is actually the better view. Far fewer crowds than Angels Landing.

## June Tips

| | |
|---|---|
| 🌅 **Start Early** | Every hike starts by 7 AM. Temps hit 90–95°F by midday. Most trails have zero shade. |
| 💧 **Water** | 2L per person minimum. Bring electrolyte tablets — water alone isn't enough on big days. |
| ☀️ **Sunscreen** | SPF 50. Reapply every 2 hours. High elevation + reflective red rock = brutal UV. |
| 📱 **Offline Maps** | Download AllTrails trails offline the night before — cell service in canyons is essentially zero. |
| ⚡ **Flash Floods** | Late June brings monsoon storms. Check NOAA before slot canyon hikes. Exit if weather builds. |
| 🚌 **Zion Shuttle** | Free shuttle runs every 5–10 min to every trailhead. No personal vehicles in Zion Canyon. |
| 🧦 **Wet Feet** | Kanarra Falls + The Narrows both mean wet feet. Rent gear from Zion Outfitter. |
| 🎟️ **Permits** | Kanarra Falls = book months ahead. Canyoneering = book now. Everything has a cap in June. |

### 🚲 E-Bike — Zion Canyon Scenic Ride
**Day 4 — Wednesday June 24 (afternoon)**

| | |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | Half day (4 hrs) recommended — afternoon after morning hike |
| **Difficulty** | Easy — pedal-assist does the work |
| **Where You Ride** | Zion Canyon Scenic Drive + Pa'rus Trail |
| **Book At** | **Zion Cycles** — zioncycles.com · 435-772-0400 |
| **Cost** | **~$100/bike** · helmets + locks included |

Ride the same road the park shuttle uses — but at your own pace, stopping whenever you want for photos, with no waiting for a bus. The Zion Canyon Scenic Drive runs 6 miles through the heart of the canyon with 2,000-ft walls on both sides and passes every major viewpoint and trailhead. The Pa'rus Trail is a 3.5-mile paved riverside path along the Virgin River that's open to bikes and pedestrians. Together they give you a completely different perspective of Zion than hiking — more ground covered, more relaxed, genuinely magical.

**Why Zion Cycles is the top pick:** They rent high-end Trek e-bikes with 9-speed gearing — most other shops rent single-speed bikes which are noticeably less smooth on the canyon road. They're also the only full-service bike shop in Springdale so bikes are well-maintained.

**⚠️ Important:** Only Class 1 pedal-assist e-bikes are allowed in Zion NP (motor assists while pedaling, cuts off at 20 mph). Throttle bikes are not permitted. All rental shops listed here are compliant.  
**⚠️ Book ahead** — June is peak season and bike inventory is limited. Reserve as soon as your dates are confirmed.

### 🌟 Zion Stargazing Tour
**Day 3 — Tuesday June 23 (evening, ~9 PM)** ✅ BOOKED

| | |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 2 hours |
| **Cost** | **~$190/person** |
| **Book At** | stargazingzion.com · 435-919-3976 |
| **Location** | ~10 miles up Kolob Terrace Rd on Zion's western boundary |

Zion is a designated International Dark Sky Park — on a clear June night with a new moon you can see the full Milky Way arching overhead. The tour starts at sunset and walks you to a private dark-sky field away from any light pollution. Each person gets a zero-gravity reclining pod and a blanket, plus Celestron binoculars. Professional astronomy guides walk you through constellations, planets, distant galaxies, and nebulae using high-powered telescopes and laser pointers. Hot chocolate, coffee, and tea included.

**Tip:** Late June 2025 new moon falls June 25 — your stargazing tour is on new moon night itself. Maximum darkness, best possible conditions.  
**⚠️ Weather dependent** — full refund if cancelled due to clouds. Book early, small group caps.
